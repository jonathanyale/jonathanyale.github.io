fusilli arrabbiata



ingredients

100g fusilli pasta
80g spicy italian sausage, casings removed
100g canned san marzano tomatoes, crushed
1/2 red bell pepper, strips
30g baby spinach
1 clove garlic, thinly sliced
1/2 dried red chili, crushed (or 1/8 tsp red pepper flakes)
1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
1/2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
20g pecorino romano, grated
salt and black pepper to taste

instructions

fill large pot (at least 3 liters) with water. add 1 tsp salt. bring to rolling boil over high heat
add fusilli pasta to boiling water. stir immediately to prevent sticking. cook according to package time minus 1 minute (usually 10-11 minutes). pasta should be firm but not crunchy when done
while pasta cooks, heat olive oil in large frying pan or skillet over medium heat (setting 5-6 on electric stove)
add sausage to hot oil. use wooden spoon to break into small pieces (about grape-sized). cook 4-5 minutes until no pink remains and pieces are golden brown
add sliced garlic and crushed chili to pan. stir constantly for 30 seconds until garlic smells fragrant but not brown (don't let it burn!)
add bell pepper strips to pan. stir and cook 2-3 minutes until peppers start to soften but still have some crunch
add crushed tomatoes to pan. add pinch of salt and few grinds of black pepper. reduce heat to medium-low (setting 3-4). let bubble gently for 6-8 minutes, stirring occasionally. sauce should thicken and look less watery
add spinach leaves to pan. stir until leaves shrink and wilt completely (about 1 minute)
before draining pasta: use ladle or measuring cup to scoop 1/4 cup pasta cooking water. set aside. then drain pasta in colander
add drained pasta to sauce pan. add 2-3 tablespoons of reserved pasta water. toss everything together vigorously with tongs or large spoon for 1-2 minutes until pasta is well coated
remove pan from heat. add parsley and half the grated cheese. toss once more
serve immediately in bowl. top with remaining cheese and extra black pepper. eat while hot!

notes

arrabbiata means "angry" - adjust heat level to preference
pasta water starch helps sauce cling to fusilli spirals
use best quality tomatoes you can find
nutritionally balanced for active young male: ~820 calories, 35g protein
sausage provides lean protein, vegetables add fiber and vitamins
bell pepper and spinach boost antioxidants and micronutrients

beginner tips

never add oil to pasta water - it prevents sauce from sticking
always save pasta water before draining - the starch helps bind sauce
don't overcook garlic - it turns bitter when brown
taste and adjust salt/pepper at the end
if sauce seems dry, add more pasta water gradually
if too salty, add a bit more tomatoes or pasta water

variations

substitute chicken sausage for lower fat option
add mushrooms for extra umami and nutrients
include zucchini strips for additional vegetables
substitute turkey sausage for leaner protein
add fresh basil instead of parsley for different flavor profile

equipment needed

large pot (at least 3 liters) for pasta
large frying pan or skillet
colander for draining
wooden spoon for stirring
ladle or measuring cup
tongs or large spoon for mixing
grater for cheese (if not pre-grated)
